Child Safety Standards

Zita is committed to maintaining a safe environment for all users and has a zero-tolerance policy toward the exploitation or abuse of children in any form.

Prohibition of Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation

Zita explicitly prohibits all forms of Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) on its platform. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) — the creation, sharing, or distribution of any sexual content involving minors
  • Grooming behaviours — any attempt by an adult to gain the trust of a minor for exploitative purposes
  • Solicitation — requests for sexual content or in-person contact of a sexual nature involving a minor
  • Trafficking — any activity that facilitates the commercial sexual exploitation of a child
  • Any other conduct that sexualises, endangers, or exploits individuals under the age of 18
Zero Tolerance

Any account found to be engaging in CSAE-related activity will be permanently removed and reported to the relevant authorities and the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC).

User Reporting Mechanism

Zita provides an in-app mechanism that allows any user to report content or behaviour they believe violates these standards. Reports are reviewed promptly and appropriate action is taken, including content removal, account suspension, and referral to law enforcement where required.

To report a concern directly, users may also contact our child safety point of contact listed below.

Addressing Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)

Zita has procedures in place to detect, remove, and report CSAM in accordance with applicable law. Upon identification of any suspected CSAM, Zita will:

  • Immediately remove the content from the platform
  • Suspend the responsible account pending investigation
  • Report the material to NCMEC's CyberTipline and cooperate fully with law enforcement

Legal Compliance

Zita complies with all applicable child safety laws and regulations, including but not limited to the PROTECT Our Children Act, and cooperates with authorities in any investigation involving the safety of minors.
